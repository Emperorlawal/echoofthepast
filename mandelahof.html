Nelson Mandela

Country: South Africa

Date of Birth: July 18, 1918

Date of Death: December 5, 2013

Estimated Net Worth: $10 million (at time of death)

What He Did for Africa

Nelson Mandela fought against apartheid — a cruel system where Black South Africans were treated unfairly just because of their skin color. He spent 27 years in prison for standing up for justice and freedom. After he was released, instead of seeking revenge, Mandela helped bring peace and unity to South Africa.

Major Achievements

  • Led the anti-apartheid movement in South Africa.
  • Spent 27 years in prison as a political prisoner.
  • Won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993 (shared with F.W. de Klerk).
  • Became the first Black president of South Africa in 1994.
  • Promoted peace, forgiveness, and national unity.

Legacy

Mandela is remembered as a global symbol of peace, courage, and equality. His life teaches us that change is possible through patience, dialogue, and love. Even after death, his influence lives on through the Mandela Foundation and the global celebration of Mandela Day (July 18), which encourages people to help others.

Fun Fact: People around the world affectionately called him “Madiba,” which was his Xhosa clan name.
